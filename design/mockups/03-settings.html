Settings Surface — Five Explorations

How the bottom-right gear exposes two scopes — Meeting (the open note: recording, language, in-person, calendar link, vocab boost) and General (hotkey, login, theme, storage, permissions, model, vocabulary) — inside one Raycast-Notes-style floating window.

1Gear Popover

The note stays front-and-center and fully active; clicking the gear opens a transient ~320px popover anchored to it with an arrow. A segmented control flips between the most-used Meeting controls and General essentials, and “All Settings…” is the escape hatch into the full surface. This matches how menu-bar utilities and Raycast itself handle quick configuration.

Tradeoffs: only ~5 controls fit per scope, deep settings always cost a second hop, and a popover over the footer can occlude the recording timer.

2Morph In-Window

Raycast-style: the whole window content morphs into a settings page within the same chrome — an icon sidebar (General, Meeting, Recording, Vocabulary, Permissions) and a content pane, with a back-chevron returning to the note. Every setting gets full breathing room and the two scopes become peers in one navigation model.

Tradeoffs: the note disappears entirely, which is heavy for a one-toggle errand and risky mid-meeting; sidebar nav is overkill for the ~12 settings the app has today.

3Bottom Sheet

A sheet slides up from the footer to ~70% of the window, dimming the note behind it — the meeting context stays visibly “underneath” the settings that affect it. Grouped, rounded macOS-form cards scroll comfortably, so both scopes can show everything without truncation, and the grabber affords a quick swipe-away dismissal.

Tradeoffs: covers most of the note while open, and bottom sheets read slightly iOS-native rather than macOS-native.

4Native Tabs, Glassy

A separate-feeling settings panel modeled on macOS System Settings — icon tabs across the toolbar (General · Meeting · Recording · Vocabulary · Permissions) — but rendered in translucent glass with vibrancy, so it belongs to the same glass family as the floating note. The most “Mac-correct” pattern: users already know toolbar tabs from every native app’s preferences.

Tradeoffs: a second window-feel dilutes the “one floating panel” promise, and glass legibility lives and dies by what happens to be behind it.

5Footer Drawer

The lightest option: the gear expands the footer upward into a ~220px quick-settings drawer while the note stays visible and editable above. Two ultra-compact columns put Meeting toggles and General shortcuts side by side — everything is one click, zero navigation — with “Open full settings ⌘,” for the rest.

Tradeoffs: very dense with small hit targets; it only fits the essentials, so a full settings surface is still required elsewhere.
