Variation 01

Palette Overlay

The classic Raycast pattern: ⌘K summons a command-palette layer floating above the (dimmed, blurred) editor inside the same window. Results are grouped by recency with an "Upcoming" group on top, so the next meeting is always one keystroke away. Zero spatial reorientation — you never leave the note you were in.

Tradeoffs: No note preview while browsing; the overlay caps how much metadata each row can carry before it gets noisy.

Variation 02

Sidebar Slide-in

⌘K slides a 260px notes list over the left edge of the window — the editor stays live and visible on the right, so switching feels like glancing at a drawer rather than entering a mode. Pinned meetings sit on top, the rest is chronological, and hovering a row reveals micro-actions (pin / delete).

Tradeoffs: Eats editor width while open and search is cramped; mouse-friendlier than keyboard-first.

Variation 03 · Light

Card Grid

⌘K swaps the window content for a searchable gallery of meeting cards — title, time, avatar stack, location chip, transcript badge and a two-line excerpt give enough scent to recognize a meeting at a glance. Built for visual scanners who remember "the Müller meeting" by its shape, not its filename. Shown light to compare day-time legibility.

Tradeoffs: Lowest information density (6–7 cards per screen) and full content swap means losing sight of the current note.

Variation 04

Agenda Timeline

The switcher doubles as a meeting-prep view: a day-grouped vertical timeline merges upcoming calendar events (dashed ghost cards with a one-click "Create note") with existing notes (solid rows), all in chronological order. You see what's next and what happened in one scan — switching and preparing become the same gesture.

Tradeoffs: Needs calendar permission to shine; the rail costs horizontal space and pure text search feels secondary to time-based browsing.

Variation 05

Mini Bar + Preview

Spotlight-minimal: a compact centered search bar in the upper third, five tight result rows under it, and a live preview pane showing the selected meeting's frontmatter plus the first lines of markdown. Maximum keyboard speed — type, arrow once or twice, confirm with the preview, hit return.

Tradeoffs: Only five visible results forces good ranking; preview pane is wasted space on tiny screens.
