Settings v2 — Bottom Sheet, Refined ×5

Round 1's winner, pushed further. Every variation is a bottom sheet sliding up inside the compact Raycast-Notes-shaped window (~420 × 620). What varies: sheet height, internal structure, and how the Meeting / General scopes are switched. Use the Glass / Solid toggle above each window to compare materials.

1Classic Sheet

The straight refinement of the round-1 winner: a ~72%-height glass sheet with a grabber, a segmented [Meeting | General] switch centered at the top, and scrollable grouped cards beneath. One surface, two scopes, zero navigation depth — the note title stays visible above the sheet so you never lose context.

Tradeoff: at 72% the note is mostly covered, and both scopes share one scroll area so long sections push controls below the fold.

2Half Sheet + Expand

A ~45% 'quick' sheet for mid-meeting tweaks: only the four most-used Meeting controls, instantly reachable, with most of the note still readable above. "All settings ⌘," at the bottom (with an upward chevron) is the gesture/click affordance into the full sheet. Optimized for speed while a call is live.

Tradeoff: every General-scope change is two steps away, and users may never discover the expand row if they only skim.

3Sheet with Icon Tabs

A future-proof ~85% sheet that replaces the two-scope segmented control with a row of System-Settings-style icon tabs: General · Meeting · Recording · Vocabulary · Permissions. Each tab is a short, fully visible pane — no long scrolls — so the surface scales as NoteTakr grows. Shown with Permissions active; tabs are clickable.

Tradeoff: five tabs is heavy chrome for today's handful of settings, and the Meeting/General mental split gets diluted across tabs.

4Two-Stage Peek

One sheet, two detents — shown here as two windows. Stage A is a slim ~120px peek docked above the footer with the three live-meeting toggles; drag the grabber (or hit ⌘,) and it becomes Stage B, the full ~75% sheet with everything. The same surface serves both the two-second tweak and the deep dive.

Tradeoff: drag-to-detent is an iOS gesture that macOS users rarely attempt with a mouse, so the expand path needs a visible affordance too.

5Solid Sheet

Structurally identical to #1, but it defaults to a solid #1c1c1e window and sheet — maximum text contrast, no wallpaper bleed-through behind small gray labels. When flipped to Glass, the sheet stays slightly more opaque than the other variations for legibility. Shown with the General scope active so the full second scope is visible somewhere on this page.

Tradeoff: loses the airy glass identity that makes the floating window feel like part of the desktop, reading closer to a plain dark panel.
