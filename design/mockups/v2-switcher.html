⌘K Switcher — Agenda Timeline, refined ×5

Round 1 winner was the Agenda Timeline. Per your request, every variation here keeps one core behavior: when ⌘K opens, the meeting note blurs in place and the timeline floats on top of it — same compact portrait window, Raycast-Notes-sized (420 × 620). The five takes vary only the container, density, and which edge the layer comes from.

Variation 1

Full Frost

The timeline takes over the entire window interior: the open note frosts into a soft backdrop and the switcher becomes the window. This is the most Raycast-like reading — one surface, zero chrome ambiguity, and the blurred note underneath still whispers "you haven't left your document."

Tradeoff: with the note fully covered, you lose the spatial cue of where the switcher sits relative to your text.

Variation 2

Floating Card

A slightly inset glass card floats centered over the lightly blurred note — the note's edges stay visible all around it, so the layering is unmistakable: your document is still there, the switcher just hovers above it. Hover a row to reveal pin/trash micro-actions (shown on the "1:1 Sarah" row).

Tradeoff: the inset costs ~30px of list width and height in an already narrow window.

Variation 3

Drop From Top

The timeline drops out of the chrome like a sheet pinned to the title bar — rounded bottom corners only, covering about three quarters of the window while the blurred note peeks out below. The search field moves into the chrome row, replacing the window title while the switcher is open, which saves a full row of height.

Tradeoff: search-in-chrome collides with the traffic lights' drag region and is unusual for macOS muscle memory.

Variation 4

Rise From Bottom

A bottom sheet rises from the footer — grabber handle, rounded top corners, search at its top — while the blurred note title peeks out above. This matches the settings bottom-sheet language, so the whole app speaks one system: layers slide in from window edges and your note never goes away, it just recedes.

Tradeoff: the search field sits mid-window rather than top, which feels less launcher-like than Full Frost.

Variation 5

Dense Solid

Same Full Frost layout, but the window defaults to solid dark #1c1c1e (toggle back to glass if you like) and the list is tuned for speed: tighter rows, sticky day headers while scrolling, and no icons — just small status dots (red = current, green = has transcript). Maximum rows on screen, fastest keyboard feel.

Tradeoff: losing icons removes the at-a-glance Meet / in-person / note distinction; dots carry less meaning.
