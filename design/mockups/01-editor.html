Raycast Twin

Keeps the exact visual language of Raycast Notes so the app reads instantly as "notes," not "meeting tool." The single addition is a one-line frontmatter strip under the title that expands into an Obsidian-style property table on demand. Recording stays ambient — a quiet red pill in the strip, never a banner.

Tradeoffs: metadata is invisible by default, so it's easy to forget a recording is running or that the note is calendar-linked.

Interactive — click the frontmatter strip to expand the property table.

Glass

Leans into macOS 26 liquid glass so the panel feels like a system surface floating over your meeting, not another app window. Frontmatter becomes a row of touchable chips — each one is both display and control: toggle in-person, click REC to stop, click the avatar stack for the attendee list. Vibrancy keeps the window weightless even with metadata always on screen.

Tradeoffs: legibility depends on the wallpaper behind it, and chips cap how much metadata fits before the row wraps.

Interactive — click the In-person chip to flip the toggle.

3 Variation

Paper

A light, paper-warm surface optimized for writing during and reading after the meeting — print-like contrast and generous margins. The property table is always visible but typographically quiet, exactly like Obsidian properties. Recording is reduced to a heartbeat in the footer: a thin waveform and a timer, never interrupting the page.

Tradeoffs: a light panel stands out hard against dark macOS setups, and the always-on table costs ~140px of writing space.

4 Variation

Split Live

Transcription as a first-class citizen: you write on the left while the diarized transcript streams on the right, so you can quote, verify, and back-fill without leaving the note. The red header strip makes recording state impossible to miss, with Stop one click away. The transcript collapses to a thin rail when you just want to write.

Tradeoffs: the densest of the five — at 720px each pane gets narrow, and the recording strip spends vertical space for the whole meeting.

Interactive — click the chevron in the transcript header to collapse the pane.

Zen + ⓘ

The strongest possible statement of focus: a black sheet, your words, nothing else — chrome only materializes on hover (the traffic-light corner is shown in its hover state). All meeting metadata hides behind a single ⓘ that surfaces a glass card over the bottom of the note. Recording is a 2px ember along the top edge: visible in your periphery, ignorable in your focus.

Tradeoffs: everything is hidden, so discoverability suffers — new users may never find the metadata or notice they're recording.

Interactive — click the ⓘ button (bottom-right) to open the metadata overlay.
