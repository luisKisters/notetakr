Variation 1

Quiet Twin

The Glass Twin stripped to essentials: no window title, no word count, no format button — just dimmed lights and a gear that fades in on hover. Metadata is one muted line under the H1; click it for a plain hairline property list, and the footer holds nothing but the three views.

Tradeoff: the gear and the expandable metadata are nearly invisible until you already know they exist.

Variation 2

Hairline Grid

Same skeleton as Quiet Twin, but the footer becomes a literal three-cell grid: hairline top border, two hairline verticals, one centered label per cell — the whole cell is the click target. The active cell carries a 4% purple wash and a brighter label instead of an underline.

Tradeoff: a tint that faint can read as a hover state rather than a selection on busy wallpapers.

Variation 3

Almost Nothing

How far can restraint go: metadata collapses to three mute icons plus the REC timer (values live in the expanded list), footer tabs are bare words separated by nothing but space, and the gear exists only while the cursor is over the window. A stress test for the minimal ceiling.

Tradeoff: icons without values force a click — or memory — to recall the time, place, and headcount.

Variation 4

Light Quiet

Variation 1 defaulting to the Light theme: warm paper white, near-black ink, the same sparing purple — proof the Glass / Dark / Light trio holds together as one design language. Flip the toggle to confirm nothing about the layout depends on darkness.

Tradeoff: glass is the brand; light mode must stay a second skin, not drift into looking like a different app.
