Variation 1

Glass Twin — Tabs in Footer

The Raycast Twin you picked, rebuilt in liquid glass. The top stays calm — title plus one collapsible chip strip (click it to expand the Obsidian-style property table). Tabs move into the footer as a centered segmented control, keeping the writing surface completely chrome-free.

Tradeoff: footer gets dense — word count must shrink and move left, and tab labels are small touch targets.

Variation 2

Tabs Above Frontmatter

Tabs as the very first thing in the body — a full-width glass segmented bar right under the chrome, above the H1. This makes the three views feel like equal siblings (like browser tabs), and the footer stays exactly like Raycast: centered word count only.

Tradeoff: a persistent bar above the title costs ~36px of always-on chrome and pushes the H1 down on every note.

Variation 3

Tabs Below Frontmatter — Full-Width Pills

Your "pills take in all width" request: under the H1 sits a 2×2 edge-to-edge grid of equal-width metadata pills (time, location, participants, REC), each with an icon and a secondary line. Tabs follow as a full-width underline row — the calmest tab style, no boxes.

Tradeoff: the pill grid is the tallest frontmatter of the five (~70px) and is shown only in its collapsed state here.

Variation 4

One Full-Width Meta Bar

All frontmatter condensed into a single stretched pill spanning the window — items separated by dots, a chevron at the right end expands the full property table (shown expanded by default here; click the bar to collapse). Tabs become small icon pills centered in the footer.

Tradeoff: one line can't fit every field at 420px, so participants collapse to a count until expanded.

Variation 5

Solid Dark Twin

Variation 1's layout, but defaulting to the solid #1c1c1e Raycast look (flip the toggle to see it as glass) — proof the design survives both materials. The top is the most minimal of all: frontmatter is one muted text line, no chips, no borders.

Tradeoff: the plain text line gives no affordance that the metadata is interactive or expandable.
