⌘K Switcher — the Agenda Timeline, back for real

Round 1's winner, kept literally: the vertical timeline line, node dots per item, day rail, and the ghost "Create note" entry — now wearing the round-3 identity (purple accent, glass / dark / light, no pills, no clutter). The frost layer is fixed: blur 40px + saturate 1.5 with a lighter fill and a 1px top highlight, so it reads as crisp glass over the blurred note instead of fog. Toggle each window between the three themes.

Variation 1

Frost Timeline

Round one's agenda timeline, unchanged — same line, same dots, same day rail, same ghost entry — rendered as a single full-window frost layer over the open note. The blur is doubled and the fill lightened versus round 2, so the layer reads like crisp glass with the note and desktop color glowing faintly through, not mud.

Tradeoff: glass legibility still depends on what sits behind the window — over a very busy desktop the Dark theme will always read better.

Variation 2

Solid Timeline

The identical layout, defaulting to the solid Dark theme: purple-tinted near-black #151417, no transparency between you and the list, and the timeline line plus hairlines stepped up a notch so structure stays visible without glass depth. This is the fastest-feeling read and the one for people who turn translucency off system-wide.

Tradeoff: the opaque surface loses the "your note is still right there" layering cue that the frost gives.

Variation 3

Breathing Timeline

Same structure with more air: small-caps day labels, taller rows, and a timeline line that fades in and out at its ends instead of running hard edge-to-edge. The selected entry breathes open to reveal one quiet metadata line — time · place · people — with no pills or badges anywhere.

Tradeoff: fewer items per screen, so a long meeting history means noticeably more scrolling.
